Purpose of IT in modern business

I was thinking about how from the early 1980s with the first desktop computers, information technology has played an important part in global economies. Companies rely on IT for fast communications, data processing and market intelligence. IT plays an integral role in every industry, helping companies improve business processes, achieve cost efficiencies, drive revenue growth and maintain a competitive advantage in the marketplace.

  • Product Development

    IT speed up the time it takes new products to reach the market. From innovations in microprocessors to efficient drug delivery systems, information technology helps businesses respond quickly to changing customer requirements.

  • Stakeholder Integration

    IT Helps companies to run 24/7 using call centres abroad, use investor relations websites to communicate with shareholders, research analysts and other market participants.

  • Improving a Process

    Enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems allow managers to review sales, costs and other operating metrics on one integrated software platform, usually in real time. ERP system may replace  functional areas of the business a.g HR, Sales, Accountancy.

  • Cost Efficient

    Computers are getting more and more powerful and cheaper, and mobile, it allows  companies to reduce transaction and implementation costs.  New solutions help save money from postage to losing a crop for farmers due to the weather. Advertising becoming more cost efficient. IT help maintain and improve product quality and customer service

  • Competitive Advantage

    Cost savings, rapid product development and process improvements help companies gain and maintain a competitive advantage in the marketplace. Rapid prototyping, software simulations and other IT-based systems help to bring a product to market cost effectively and quickly

Access issues that need taking into account issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

Blindness. Hard to access the visual content. With proper website code such as HTML providing textual equivalent to visual information, blind people can access information with the use of text-to-Braille hardware or text-to-speech software

Mobility disabilities such as difficulty to use hands, tremor, loss of fine motor skills are obstacles to navigating the web. But when websites are coded in a way to allow access and navigation via keyboard or single switch device such as joystick make browsing ehe web easy

Hearing impairment and deafness. Some audio formats could include closed captioning or sign language

Epilepsy. Flashing effects can trigger seizures in people who have epilepsy. Solution is prior warning for people with epilepsy or an option to turn off the flashing effects

Cognitive/intellectual disabilities. People with a disability which affects memory, attention, ability to learn, solve problems, etc. often experience difficulties understanding the communicated information due to complexity of website design or its content. But solutions such as readability, user friendly navigation, consistency, colour contrast, etc. which help them navigate the website and understand its content do not affect usability for non-disabled people.

Legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

GDPR regulates how companies store data. The practice of collecting email contacts for marketing, sales purposes or even designing user account registration forms are restricted to the minimum. The breach of GDPR regulations can lead to serious consequences such as financial lost and company image fall

For example, developer should know about “data protection by design and by default,” referring to the implementation of appropriate technical measures for ensuring that only personal data which is necessary for each specific purpose of the processing is processed. This applies to the amount of data collected, the extent of its processing, the period of its retention as well as its accessibility. It is important to plan a system of logs that will track what happens with stored data—who and when enters, modifies, erases, accesses them.
